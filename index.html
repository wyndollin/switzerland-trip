Reading Notes for the Couple

Tap water = free Evian.

Every public fountain in Switzerland is drinking water by default. Refill bottles. A 1L bottle of water at a kiosk is 4 CHF — that's a sandwich.

Coop & Migros close at 7 PM.

Earlier on Saturdays, closed Sundays. Stock up on Saturday morning. Coop Pronto kiosks at train stations stay open later for emergencies.

The pass is on your phone.

PDF on screen is fine. Inspectors scan the QR. Screenshot it in case of no signal in mountain tunnels.

Right side of the train.

Zürich → Interlaken: right side has lake views (Lake Thun). Schynige Platte cogwheel: right side for the Jungfrau panorama. Lake Thun boat: top deck.

Reserve Oeschinensee.

The boarding-pass time slot is required in summer for public-transport visitors. Free to reserve, free to rebook. Don't show up without one.

Weather is god here.

If Day 1 is rainforest-grey, swap Schynige Platte for the next sunny day and do something low-altitude (Bern). Mountain views in clouds are wasted money.

Currency: pay CHF, not EUR.

Many places accept EUR but the rate is brutal. Use a debit card with no foreign fees (Revolut, N26, BBVA Aqua) and pay in CHF every time.

The trains run on time.

If your connection says 4 minutes, you have 4 minutes. SBB punctuality is real. Don't relax until you're seated on the next train.
